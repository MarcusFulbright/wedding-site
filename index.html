Marcus

Fulbright

Marcus is a Seattle native who moved to Nashville TN to attend Belmont University, where he would first meet Steph. After getting his degree in Music Business, Marcus left the entertainment industry to pursue a career in technology. He attended Nashville Software School's inaugural class to learn about programming and web development. Since completing NSS in 2012, Marcus has worked for DOD contracts, Health Care companies, and everything in between.

When not building the internet, Marcus enjoys craft beer, whiskey of all varieties, pool, NBA Basketball (Go Blazers), and listening to Heavy Metal. Sharing these things with Steph has been a true joy.

Stephanie

Downing

Steph grew up in Arlington, MA, right out side of Boston. She moved to the South to go to Belmont University for undergrad, because she couldn't find a good college in the Boston area. She graduated with a History and a Business degree, and went on to work for HCA (Hospital Corporation of America) for 3 years in their IT department as a project manager and business analyst. In 2015, she went back to school to get her Master's of Theological Studies in Public History of Religion at Vanderbilt University. She is working on her thesis and is graduating in May.

In between papers and reading, Steph is an avid Boston Bruins (hockey) and theater fan. She loves baking and nearly all things British. She has loved getting to introduce Marcus to hockey and the medieval history of England and Ireland (did you know the Irish invented lower case letters?).

Steph curated an exhibit at Vanderbilt's Divinity Library on Syriac culture in the Middle East and Asia. You can go see what she's been working on at Vanderbilt.
You can also look at www.VisitMusicCity.com or the list of Bride & Groom approved attractions below

Downtown Area

The Downtown area has a rich country music history. Several Honkey-Tonks and Dives were standard hang outs for Opry stars. There's plenty to do while walking around, and you can always find a cold drink and live music any time of the day

  • Recommended Spots:
  • Tootsies
  • Rippy's
  • Mike's Ice Cream
  • Fleet Street
  • Printer's Alley
  • The Arcade
  • Pedestrian Bridge

  • Grand Ole Opry

  • Country Music Hall Of Fame
  • George Jones Museum
  • Johnny Cash Museum
  • RCA Studio B
  • Music Row

Hot Chicken

If you love fried chicken then you have to try the Nashville original: Hot Chicken. Pick your spice level (hot like the groom or not like the bride) get you some sides and prepare for an experience. Lots of places in Nashville claim to serve hot chicken, but according to the Groom, only the following are considered bonafide

  • Recommended Spots:
  • Hattie B's
  • PepperFire
  • Boltons
  • Prince's
